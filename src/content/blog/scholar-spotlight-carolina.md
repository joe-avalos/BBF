Carolina Reyes was seventeen when she first heard about Building Baja's Future through a teacher at her high school in Los Cabos. She had maintained a near-perfect grade point average throughout secondary school, but like many students in the region, the cost of university tuition seemed out of reach.

"I knew I wanted to study something that would help my community," Carolina says. "But I didn't think I had a way to get there."

## Finding Her Path

Carolina applied to BBF in the spring of 2022. She was one of over 80 applicants that year and one of 12 students selected to receive a scholarship. Her focus: environmental engineering at the Autonomous University of Baja California Sur (UABCS) in La Paz.

The move to La Paz was her first time living away from home. BBF's support network made the transition manageable — from English classes twice a week, to a mentor who helped her navigate university bureaucracy, to the scholar community she found through BBF-organized events.

## Life as a BBF Scholar

For Carolina, the scholarship was only part of what BBF provided. She describes the Tree of Life program — BBF's eight-branch support model — as something that shaped her not just academically but as a person.

"The life skills sessions were things I didn't expect to need, but I did. Budgeting, professional communication, dealing with stress. You don't learn those things in class."

She completed a summer internship with a regional environmental consulting firm in 2024, an opportunity she connected to through BBF's work internship program.

## Looking Ahead

Carolina is now in her final year and expects to graduate in December 2026. She has already begun the application process for a graduate program in marine resource management.

"BBF didn't just give me tuition. They gave me a community and a foundation I'll carry for the rest of my life."
