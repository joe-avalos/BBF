## How to Apply — Selection Process

1. **Personal Interview** — Candidates who meet all requirements are invited to an individual interview with the BBF Executive Director.
2. **Knowledge Exam** — A standardized test is administered after the interview.
3. **Aptitude Test & Vocational Seminar** — Selected candidates participate in aptitude assessments and a vocational seminar.
4. **Results Notification** — Candidates are notified by June 15 whether they have been accepted or not, with an explanation.
5. **Commitment Letter** — Accepted scholars confirm their acceptance and sign a commitment letter.
6. **Welcome Ceremony** — In July, a general scholars meeting is held to deliver red t-shirts, welcome new scholars, and provide additional instructions.

---

## How to Apply — Scholar Obligations

1. Complete your university degree within the program's stipulated timeframe at an officially recognized institution. This period cannot be extended for any reason.
2. Maintain a minimum GPA of 8.5 out of 10 each trimester or semester until graduation.
3. Submit grades to the BBF Executive Director at the end of each semester or quarter.
4. Complete at least 60 hours of community service each academic year with a pre-approved project.
5. Attend a minimum of 2 hours of English classes per week, held Saturdays at BBF facilities. Mandatory and free.
6. Participate in all BBF-convened activities, provided they do not conflict with your academic schedule.
7. Any absence must be approved by the BBF Executive Director.
8. The scholarship is granted for the current school year only. It is renewed if all obligations are met. BBF may cancel the scholarship at any time per its regulations.
