### OUR STORY PAGE

**English:**
- Overview: "Building Baja's Future (BBF) is a nonprofit organization based in Baja California Sur, Mexico that awards scholarships to deserving young people for college education." BBF provides "well-rounded programs where their talents are recognized, nurtured, and strengthened." Beyond financial aid, BBF identifies "areas of life and learning that some of its scholars missed out on."
- History:
  - 2006: Cecilia Portilla and Jacinto Avalos, along with two friends, began offering scholarships to Los Cabos college students
  - 2008: Over 30 scholars had received financial assistance
  - 2012: BBF was formally incorporated and began specialized programming
- The BBF Tree of Life (eight support areas):
  1. Current Technology - Training in new technology and software
  2. Economic Assistance - Support for transportation, housing, seminars, books, and supplies
  3. Artistic Expression - Classes in dance and self-expression
  4. Emotional Help - Support navigating challenges
  5. Health Assistance and Physical Activity - Yoga, swimming, and medical assistance
  6. Cultural Life - Access to concerts and performances
  7. Psychological Help - Counseling and therapy services
  8. Social Life - Social activities, outings, celebrations
- Logo meaning: The hand in the BBF logo represents "both our past -- our DNA -- and our future." The design references "cave paintings from pre-historic Baja California" while maintaining modern artistic interpretation.
- Annual Report 2017 PDF available for download.

### HOW TO APPLY PAGE

**English:**
- Application Deadline: March 30th annually
- Eligibility Requirements:
  - Financial need (verified through socioeconomic research)
  - Complete high school with minimum GPA of 9 (out of 10)
  - Leadership in school and community activities
  - Desire to attend college
  - Willingness to contribute to community and BBF
  - Role model potential
  - Must pursue bachelor's degree at institutions within Baja California Sur
- Scholar Obligations:
  - Maintain minimum GPA of 8.5/10 each quarter/semester
  - Complete college within program timeframe
  - Submit school records after each term
  - Complete minimum 60 community service hours annually with documentation
  - Attend minimum 2 hours weekly Saturday English lessons
  - Participate in BBF activities (with director approval for absences)
  - Scholarship renews annually based on meeting all obligations
  - BBF reserves right to cancel scholarships if standards not maintained
- Application Steps (7):
  1. Download application form
  2. Complete all sections in black ink or typed; sign with delivery date
  3. Attach recent passport-size photograph
  4. Provide high school transcript (GPA 9.0+)
  5. Include teacher recommendation letter
  6. Deliver to BBF office (Mon-Fri 9-16:00, Sat 9-14:00)
  7. In-person delivery encouraged
- Selection Process (3 stages):
  1. Interview with BBF Director (for complete applications)
  2. Aptitude testing and vocational seminar for selected candidates
  3. Notification by June 15th of scholarship awards
- Application form download: https://buildingbajasfuture.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/Solicitud-BBF-version-3.pdf
